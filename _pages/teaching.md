I taught a course on geometric group theory in the winter semester of 2025/6, the notes for which are can be found [here](https://lawk-mineh.github.io/files/course/Geometric_Group_Theory.pdf). It still requires some editing to insert references and so on, and maybe at some point I will add pictures. Comments and corrections are always appreciated. Below you can find a breakdown of the notes in terms of what I covered in each lecture, which may differ in some respects from the above document.

  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_1.pdf">Lecture 1</a>: Group presentations, actions, Cayley graphs, and quasi-isometries.</li>

  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_4.pdf">Lecture 4</a>: Quasi-isometric invariance of hyperbolicity, local quasigeodesics in hyperbolic spaces, quasiconvexity.</li>
  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_5.pdf">Lecture 5</a>: The boundary of a hyperbolic metric space, isometries of hyperbolic metric spaces.</li>
  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_6.pdf">Lecture 6</a>: Hyperbolic groups, centralisers of elements, and finite subgroups.</li>

  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_12.pdf">Lecture 12</a>: The Rips construction, quasiconvex subgroups, and classifying spaces for hyperbolic groups.</li>

  <li><a href="https://lawk-mineh.github.io//files/course/Lecture_14.pdf">Lecture 14</a>: Bass--Serre theory: theory, examples, and applications, accessibility theory.</li>
